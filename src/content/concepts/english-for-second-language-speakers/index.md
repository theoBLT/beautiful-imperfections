The native language of Indonesians is either their regional language (e.g. Javanese, Balinese, Batak), and/or Indonesian. Yet, *everywhere* around town, in places clearly not targeting expats, corporations decide to use English to address the locals.

A design choice that reinforces many subtle messages:

- Indonesian is a lower language. English elevates the point we're making.
- If you can read this: you're one of us. You're part of the 5-10% who can read and write such complex sentences in a foreign language.
- If you're part of the 70% in the country who can't: it doesn't matter. The point here is English as a statement, not what the text says.

What I find *even* more interesting is what I tend to consider as an elitist, exclusive approach to addressing people, is _very often_ flawed, with obvious spelling and grammar mistakes any keen eye would notice. I find fascinating this attempt to exclude through language, juxtaposed to self-ridicule that neither group notices.
