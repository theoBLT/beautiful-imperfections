The vast majority of Indonesians are Asian, dark asian, or black looking.
The vast majority of Indonesian ads feature extremely pale asians or white people.

This may seem funny, and expected (the grass is always greener, the Western world *also* primarily feature tanned advertising idols) — but it's more perverse here: it sends an implicit message to *almost* all Indonesians that their skin is at best an impediment to their success. And when featuring foreigners, that those *must* be superior, if they're chosen to be featured in local ads and locals aren't.

I truly hope this changes one day. Until then, documenting the most egregious examples I come across.
