I see posters everywhere in Indonesia, from large banners to small notes. They often state what I can, or cannot do. A laminated sheet asks me to pay attention going up the escalator. A giant LED screen in the SCBD thanks me for not doing drugs.

It's hard to believe those posters create *any* behavior changes in the reader. But that doesn't matter: the act of displaying the poster is the point — the larger, the more official-looking, the better.

## Observations

1. **Official symbols** — The blason of the city of Jakarta top right. Making it look official.

2. **In-your-face typography** — Red, bold, high-contrast fonts with a dark outline and exclamation marks. This shall not be missed.

3. **The small print becomes the big print** — 3/4th of the poster is used to outline that this rule is in accordance with **Minister of Trade Regulation No. 40 of 2022** on the amendment of Minister of Trade Regulation No. 18 of 2021 regarding **prohibited export and import goods**. Fascinating that the *reference* to the law here takes more space on the banner than the instructions themselves.
