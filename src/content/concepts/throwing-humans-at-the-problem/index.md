When faced with a problem to solve, options include software, automation, processes, technology, and if all else fails — humans. In Indonesia, I've often found that going for the scalable solution is less desirable, in large part because human capital is so cheap and willing.

This creates situations which would seem quaint, outdated or unnecessary to an outside eye, but are very much part of the day-to-day here.
